Police Shootings, Crime, and Race in the United States

An analysis of various associations between police shootings, race, and crime rates in the US from 2015-2022

Anthony Sawaya

The United States has become an increasingly tumultuous nation over
the past few years and decades, with many social issues being brought
to the forefront of public dialogue. Prominent amongst these issues is
police brutality. This visualisation will analyse police shootings over
the last 7 years, with a goal to identify if these shootings are associated
with reasonable or unreasonable factors. We will see how police shootings
stack up by state, race, and crime statistics.

Shootings vs State

This map shows the number of police shootings in each state, normaised by population

Arizona has the highest relative number of shootings, with 243 people for every 100000 having been killed by a police officer in the last 7 years.

On the opposite side of the spectrum is Vermont, with only 7 shootings per 100000 people, 35 times less than that of Arizona
